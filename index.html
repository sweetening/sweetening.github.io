About Me

In Spring 2020, after 17 years of work experience in the hospitality industry and a Bachelor of Music degree in classical voice performance from San Francisco State University, I pivoted my career and in pursuit of a career in web development by joining Le Wagon Berlin for a 9 week intensive bootcamp. I studied Ruby, Ruby on Rails, Javascript, HTML5, CSS3, SCSS, SQLite, PostgreSQL, git, Github, Heroku, REST, and Test Driven Development. During that time I built my first apps and in the process discovered my passion for creating digital products, nurtured my creativity in web design, and expanded my knowledge of programming languages.


I continued my studies with University of Helsinki's Full Stack Open 2020 program, a Javascript-based web development course focusing on React, Redux, node.js, MongoDB, GraphQL, and Typescript.


Presently I am working at Spread Group in Leipzig, Germany as a junior software engineer. I am also available for freelance work on a contractual basis.


I am always seeking opportunities to collaborate, especially with people and organizations that share a strong commitment to social causes, particularly those that create opportunities for trans and nonbinary people and women globally. If you would like to speak more about building a website or app together, please get in touch via the form below!

Recent Works

Fantastax

Fantastax is a responsive React app built with Styled Components and CSS Modules. Part of my "Frontend Faceoff" for Spreadshirt's April Hack Days, in which I compare different styling methods.

Twocycle

Twocycle is a mobile Rails app designed to promote the circular economy by connecting users who wish to repair or recycle broken electronics with bicycle couriers and local repair shops or recycling facilities. Built for Athena Hack 2021!

Welcome

A Rails web app designed to help working professionals relocating to Berlin by providing useful articles, resources, and connections with mentors that have already made the move.

Bike Match

Bike Match is an AirBnB clone built in one week which pairs cyclists in Berlin with bikes for rent in their preferred size and style.


Personal Projects

NightCAP Brooklyn

I founded this social cycling organization for riders who aren't traditionally represented in bicycle culture. NightCAP (which stands for Cycling Against the Patriarchy) exists for the trans and non-binary community, and allied cis women who agree that life is better on two wheels.

Elliott's Girls Chorus

A pop chorus full of wild wonderful women: led, written, and produced by Elliott Abaza, bass by Felix Pastorius, and a rotating line up of singers. I sang on our album that was released digitally in September 2019.
